AQUA Experiment Data Analysis - Presentation Notes

Suggested 4-slide structure:

Slide 1 - Experiment overview
- Aim: evaluate algae growth and mussel filtration using concentration data.
- Two datasets were used: mussel filtration over 30 min, and algae growth over 24 h.
- Key formulas:
  μ = ln(N_final / N_initial) / 24
  doubling time = ln(2) / μ
  filtration model: ln(Ct) = ln(C0) - k t

Slide 2 - Algae growth results
- Overall mean growth rate μ ≈ 0.0351 h^-1.
- Overall doubling time ≈ 19.7 h.
- Highest mean μ: G3.
- Lowest mean μ: G4.
- Conclusion: algae grew in all groups; growth rate is close to a one-day doubling time.

Slide 3 - Mussel filtration results
- Concentration generally decreased over 30 min for 2000, 3000, 5000, 7000 and 10000 cells/mL.
- Most reliable patterns: 2000, 5000, 7000 and 10000 cells/mL.
- Noisy concentrations: 500, 1500 and 4000 cells/mL.
- Conclusion: mussels likely filtered algae, but some measurements were affected by mixing/sampling error.

Slide 4 - Final conclusion and improvements
- The data proves that the prototype/experiment can detect changes in algae concentration.
- Error bars show that repeatability is still a limitation.
- Improvements:
  1. Mix the sample before every reading.
  2. Repeat measurements more times.
  3. Calibrate OD sensor with blank/dark readings.
  4. Keep the optical path light-tight.
  5. Log all settings: LED brightness, path length, sampling time, and pump state.
